About Us

Welcome to the Ahmad Materials and Energy Research Group (AMERG), where we explore innovative solutions in solid-state materials, lithium-ion batteries, and computational materials science. Our mission is to contribute to sustainable energy technologies and advance the global understanding of materials science.

Research

Our group specializes in:

  • Lithium-ion Batteries
  • Solid-state Batteries
  • Solar Photovoltaics
  • Computational Materials Science
  • Machine Learning in Physical Sciences and Engineering

Biography:

Dr. Ahmad's research is focused on the design of functional materials and interfaces for energy storage and conversion. The research aims to advance renewable energy and electrify transportation through first principles understanding and design of materials for batteries and solar photovoltaics. He develops a combination of theory, computation, and data-driven methods to accelerate the design of functional materials and interfaces for solid-state lithium metal batteries.

He received numerous awards, including the Institute Silver Medal at IIT Delhi, the Bushnell Fellowship in Engineering, and the Phillips and Huang Family Fellowship in Energy at Carnegie Mellon University.

Education:

  • M.S., Mechanical Engineering, Carnegie Mellon University, 2020
